Intermediate Workouts

If you’ve mastered the beginner routine, you’re ready to take on these intermediate moves. Complete 2 sets of 10 to 15 reps of each exercise below, then move on to the next after 1 minute of rest. An alternative, more advanced approach is to complete timed rounds. For instance, complete 1 minute of each exercise and repeat the circuit twice. Compete against yourself to get just 1 or 2 more reps each time you complete the routine.


SINGLE LEG BRIDGE


The single leg bridge is a great toning exercise for your abs and thighs and it also helps to lift and sculpt the buttocks.

SQUAT


Squat work up your quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, abdominal muscles, lower back and your butt too. It promote body-wide muscle building.

PUSHUPS


Traditional pushups are beneficial for building upper body strength. They work the triceps, pectoral muscles, and shoulders.

WALKING LUNGE


Walking lunges can help increase your range of motion by helping to increase flexibility, and loosen up your hips and hamstrings.

PIKE PUSHUP


The pike push up is a great exercise for building shoulder strength and improving core stability. This exercise works your shoulders, arms, chest, back and core.

GETUP SQUAT


Jump squats increase your explosive power, improve upper and lower body strength, and burn calories faster than regular squats.

SUPERMAN


As well as strengthening your lower and upper back, this move also works your glutes and hamstrings, and increases your core strength.

PLANK WITH LEG LIFT


The plank leg lifts is a full body exercise you can do anywhere, that trains your core, legs, glutes, and shoulders.

KNEELING SIDE PLANK


As the transversus abdominus gets stronger from doing planks, it will tighten your waistline all the way around in a way that crunches never could.

Fatty Fish


Fatty fish such as salmon, sardines, tuna, and trout are high in omega-3 fatty acids. Omega-3 fatty acids can help improve liver fat levels and bring down inflammation . Try this teriyaki halibut recipe, recommended by the Canadian Liver Foundation.

Grapes Juice


Grapefruit contains antioxidants that naturally protect the liver. Studies have also shown that Antioxidants can help reduce the development of hepatic fibrosis, a harmful condition in which excessive connective tissue builds up in the liver.

Blue Berries


Blueberries help protect you against nonalcoholic liver disease, which goes hand in hand with obesity and high cholesterol. If blueberries aren't your thing, other foods rich in polyphenols include dark chocolate, olives, and plums.

Beetroot Juice


Beetroot juice helps protect the liver from oxidative damage and inflammation, all while increasing its natural detoxification enzymes. Mostly beetroot juice can protect from liver deceases, and it keeps the liver as healthy.

Nuts


Nuts consumption is nutrient-dense snacks that are also high in healthy fats. Aside from boosting cardiovascular health, nuts may also help reduce the incidence of liver disease. Of all types of nuts, walnuts are amongst the most beneficial for reducing liver disease.

Coffee


coffee has been associated with improved outcomes with chronic liver disease, hepatocellular cancer (HCC), cirrhosis, colorectal cancer, esophageal cancer, breast cancer, prostate cancer, pancreatic cancer, ovarian cancer, kidney cancer, hepatitis B virus (HBV).

Tea


Tea is widely considered to be beneficial for health, but evidence has shown that it may have particular benefits for the liver. One Japanese study found that drinking 10 cups of green tea per day was associated with improved blood markers of liver health.

Olive Oil


In relation to diet, it has been proven that olive oil can have a beneficial effect. Several studies conclude that its intake can significantly reduce the deposit of fat in the liver. A Mediterranean diet rich in olive oil also reduces the risk of nonalcoholic fatty liver disease.

Prickly Pear


the studies thus far have demonstrated that prickly pear has positive effects on the liver. Prickly pear fruit and juice may help with hangover symptoms by reducing inflammation. They may also help protect the liver from damage caused by alcohol consumption.
